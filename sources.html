PESP Report: Venture Capital and Private Equity Dominate LAUSD Contracts (April 2026)

Published by the Private Equity Stakeholder Project using UTLA public records request data. Key findings: 86% of digital instruction tool contracts ($255M) went to VC/PE-backed companies since 2022; 72% of active tech contracts ($1.6B) were authorized under Carvalho's emergency no-bid powers; $10 billion in outside vendor contracts approved since Carvalho took over.

Vendor-Specific Sources

AllHere Education — "Ed" AI Chatbot

$6.2M contract approved June 2023. Company furloughed staff June 2024. CEO Joanna Smith-Griffin arrested November 2024 for fraud. FBI raided LAUSD offices and Carvalho's home February 2026.

  • The 74 — AllHere meeting with LAUSD leaders
  • EdSource — Contract and collapse coverage
  • Board approval: LAUSD Legistar (June 2023)

Innive, Inc. — $22M Fraud Case

Hong "Grace" Peng, former LAUSD IT Manager, charged March 2026 with felony counts for allegedly directing over $22M in district contracts through an illegal pay-to-play arrangement with Innive owner.

  • LA County District Attorney — Press Release
  • NBC LA, CBS LA, LAist — Case coverage
  • LAUSD Internal Audit Report (2024)

Infosys Limited — MiSiS Student Information System

$46.5M contract for the MiSiS student information system. Referenced in LAUSD Internal Audit and California court record 23-1422.

  • LAUSD Internal Audit Report (2024)
  • CA 23-1422 — Superior Court of California
  • Courthouse News Service

ContinuumCloud / Welligent

$27.3M combined in health tech contracts. Both companies are owned by Battery Ventures, a venture capital firm that also funded Paragon Solutions (Graphite spyware), which secured an ICE contract in October 2024.

  • LA Public Press — Investigative report (March 2026)
  • PESP Report — Battery Ventures / Paragon / ICE connection (April 2026)

Amplify Education

District-wide K-8 curriculum products including mCLASS, Amplify CKLA, Amplify Science, and Boost Reading. Per UTLA public records data cited in the PESP report: $225,207,363 in total contract commitments, with $164,329,349 spent as of July 2025. Original board contract amounts were not publicly disclosed. Majority owned by the Walton Family Foundation.

  • PESP Report (April 2026) — Amplify spending data from UTLA records request
  • amplify.com/lausd — Vendor LAUSD reference page
  • LAUSD ITS Digital Learning Tools — Parent resources page

Stepping Stones Group / CBEST / Progressus Therapy

Special education and behavioral health subcontracting. All three vendor names are entities under Stepping Stones Group, owned by private equity firm Leonard Green and Partners. Together worth more than $26M in LAUSD contracts per PESP report. The company settled a wage theft class action for $4.25M in 2024.

  • PESP Report (April 2026) — Stepping Stones, Leonard Green analysis

Veritas Capital Portfolio (Cambium/Lexia, HMH/Amira)

Private equity firm Veritas Capital owns both Cambium Learning Group (Lexia Learning, Explorelearning) and Houghton Mifflin Harcourt (Amira Learning, Istation). Veritas' primary business is defense contractors; its subsidiary Peraton is building the DHS HART biometric surveillance system capable of tracking ~300 million people in real time.

  • PESP Report (April 2026) — Veritas Capital / defense contractor analysis

Solar Contracts — Ameresco / Centrica / SolarCity

$20.7M+ in solar energy contracts across 21+ schools. Approved February 2025 under California Government Code §4217.10.

  • Business Wire — Ameresco press release (May 2025)
  • Centrica press release
  • LAUSD Board Agenda (02-10-25)
  • Business Wire (October 2012) — Original SolarCity deal

IXL Learning, Seesaw, Edgenuity

Ed-tech curriculum vendors. Contract amounts not publicly disclosed in board documents.

  • PR Newswire — IXL partnership extension (October 2021)
  • Vendor reference pages: IXL.com/lausd, Seesaw.com/lausd-and-seesaw-partnership
  • LAUSD instructional technology pages

A note on data accuracy

All contracts are assigned a verification status: Verified (checked against primary board documents), Reported (from news or third-party records requests, not independently verified), or Estimated (amount estimated from comparable contracts when original was undisclosed).
