Resolution on Community Safety During Heightened Immigration Enforcement

View on Legistar

What This Vote Was

On December 16, 2025, the LAUSD board voted 6-1 to adopt a resolution addressing community safety during heightened federal immigration enforcement. The resolution directed district staff to review policies and practices to protect students and families from immigration enforcement on or near school property.

Board member Sherlett Hendy Newbill (District 1) cast the sole dissenting vote. Board member Yvonne Yang was absent. This is the only split vote in the available public record.

Why It Matters

The immigration resolution stands out for two reasons. First, it is the only vote in the available record where a board member dissented — making it the only moment of recorded public disagreement on the board in over six months of data.

Second, the resolution revealed a specific accountability failure in LAUSD’s own contracting process. Three months after the vote, investigators found that LAUSD had an active contract with Welligent — a health data company owned by Battery Ventures, an investment firm with indirect ties to ICE surveillance technology. LAUSD said it was unaware of the connection.

The district had just voted to protect students from immigration enforcement. It was simultaneously paying a vendor with ties to the infrastructure enabling that enforcement. This is what happens when contracts are approved without vendor vetting.

Note on Newbill’s dissent: Board member Newbill has not publicly explained her No vote on this resolution. Her vote makes her the only board member to cast a dissenting vote in the available public record. This site does not characterize her reasoning — only records the fact of the vote.
