IT Infrastructure — School Network Upgrade Phase 3 (198 Sites)

View on Legistar

What This Vote Was

On December 16, 2025, the board approved the third phase of a multi-year IT infrastructure upgrade covering 198 school sites. This vote is part of a broader pattern of IT infrastructure contracting that includes a $250 million master services bench approved in November 2025 — a contract covering 12 vendors simultaneously, with no public visibility into individual vendor spending.

The December 16 meeting also approved two additional IT infrastructure contracts and an immigration enforcement resolution — four significant votes in a single session, three of them unanimous.

Why It Matters

LAUSD’s IT infrastructure contracting illustrates the opacity at the heart of this site’s thesis. The $250M master services bench — the contract this upgrade feeds into — lists 12 vendors sharing a $250M ceiling with no breakdown of how much each vendor actually receives. The board approved $250M in technology spending with no public visibility into who gets what.

Meanwhile, the district was simultaneously telling teachers it couldn’t afford fair wages and sending layoff notices to over 3,000 employees — more than a third of them IT technicians.
