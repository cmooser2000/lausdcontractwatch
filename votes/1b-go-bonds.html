Authorization of $1B General Obligation Bonds Issuance

A billion dollars in facilities bonds, approved the same day as the sexual abuse bonds. Three massive financial votes in one meeting. No public discussion on any of them.

View on Legistar

What This Vote Was

On June 3, 2025 — the same meeting where the board quietly approved $500 million in sexual abuse settlement bonds — the board also authorized issuing $1 billion in general obligation bonds for school facilities. General obligation bonds are voter-approved and repaid through property taxes, separate from the district’s operating budget.

LAUSD voters approved Measure US in November 2024, a $9 billion facilities bond — the largest ever put on the ballot by LAUSD. This $1 billion issuance is the first major draw from that voter-approved pool, intended for school construction, renovation, and infrastructure upgrades.

Why It Matters

Three separate bond authorizations worth billions of dollars were approved at a single board meeting on June 3, 2025 — without meaningful public discussion of any of them.

The facilities bond itself was developed without consultation of the district’s own Bond Oversight Committee, whose chair publicly stated it had been excluded from the process. The chair said the process prevented them from providing any meaningful comment.

LAUSD has $9 billion in new voter-approved bond capacity. The question of how that money is spent — on genuine facility needs or on technology infrastructure for ed-tech vendors — is one parents should be watching.
