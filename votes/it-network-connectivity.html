IT Infrastructure — School Network Alternative Connectivity Pilot

A second IT infrastructure vote on the same day, feeding into the same $250M vendor pool where individual spending is invisible to the public.

View on Legistar

What This Vote Was

Also approved December 16, 2025 was a pilot program for school network connectivity — expanding internet infrastructure to additional school sites. This vote is part of the same IT infrastructure contracting cluster as the network upgrade vote, and feeds into the same master services bench of 12 vendors.

Why It Matters

Three IT infrastructure votes on the same day, all unanimous, all feeding into a $250M vendor pool with no public accountability for individual vendor spending. This is the procurement structure that allowed vendors like Digital Scepter — a company with 98 LinkedIn followers and no significant public profile — to appear on a $250M master services contract alongside CDW Government, a $20B revenue technology reseller.

The board’s unanimous approval without discussion means parents have no public record of anyone asking: who are these vendors, what will they actually deliver, and how will we know if they delivered it?
