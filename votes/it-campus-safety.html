December 16, 20257-0 (Yang absent)

IT Infrastructure — Campus Safety Systems Phase 2

Surveillance infrastructure approved without vetting. Three months later, the board discovered an active vendor had indirect ties to ICE surveillance technology.

View on Legistar

What This Vote Was

The third IT infrastructure vote on December 16, 2025 covers the second phase of a campus safety systems expansion — cameras, monitoring, and security infrastructure at LAUSD schools. Campus safety technology contracting has been a significant and growing area of LAUSD spending, included in the broader IT master services bench.

Why It Matters

Campus safety systems raise a specific transparency concern. Surveillance infrastructure — cameras, monitoring systems, data collection tools — involves sensitive student data and raises civil liberties questions that the board has not publicly addressed.

One vendor on LAUSD’s broader IT contracts, Welligent, was later found to have ties to Battery Ventures — an investment firm with connections to surveillance technology companies supplying tools to ICE. The board had no public discussion of vendor vetting for security-sensitive contracts.

Three months after this vote, the board passed a resolution to review contracts with vendors connected to immigration enforcement — suggesting the vetting that should have happened before approval is now happening after.
