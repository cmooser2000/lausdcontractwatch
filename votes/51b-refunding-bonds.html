Authorization of $5.1B General Obligation Refunding Bonds

The largest of three bond authorizations approved June 3 with no public comment. Together, the three votes committed over $6.6 billion in a single silent session.

View on Legistar

What This Vote Was

Also approved on June 3, 2025 was an authorization to refinance up to $5.1 billion in existing general obligation bonds. Refunding bonds are a standard financial mechanism — similar to refinancing a mortgage — that allows the district to replace older high-interest bonds with new lower-interest debt, potentially saving money over time.

This is the most technical of the three bond votes on June 3. Unlike the judgment obligation bonds, this does not represent new spending — it restructures existing debt.

Why It Matters

The June 3 board meeting approved three separate bond actions totaling over $6.6 billion in a single session, without comment or public presentation on any of them. The refunding bonds themselves are standard practice — but the pattern of approving massive financial actions without discussion is not.

When parents and taxpayers have no opportunity to hear board members discuss or question decisions of this scale, accountability disappears. The board’s silence is not neutrality — it is a choice.
