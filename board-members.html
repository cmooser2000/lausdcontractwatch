Meet the Board That Approved $6.7 Billion in Private Contracts

Seven elected members. Unanimous on almost every vote in the public record.

Transparency Gap

LAUSD's Legistar system only contains roll-call vote records from June 2025 onward. Key votes — including the AllHere AI chatbot contract, iReady renewals, and teacher contract approvals — are not publicly accessible in the digital record. LAUSD Contract Watch filed 2 public records requests for complete voting histories. LAUSD did not respond to either.

7 Elected board members
Seven people control an $18 billion budget. They are elected by district, serve four-year terms, and answer to voters — but their votes are nearly impossible to find in the public record.
7-0 Every vote unanimous except one
In over six months of available voting records, the board has never pushed back on a contract, a bond, or a spending decision. A board that never disagrees is not providing oversight.
84% Jump in ed-tech spending the year Carvalho arrived
The moment Carvalho took over in 2022, LAUSD’s commitments to education technology companies jumped 84% in a single year. Student outcomes did not.
$29M+ Lost to active criminal investigations
A $22 million pay-to-play scheme. A $6.2 million AI chatbot whose founder was indicted for fraud. Both happened while the district said it had no money for teachers.

The Only Contested Vote

In the available public record, the board voted unanimously on every item — including $500 million in bonds approved without public discussion — except one. On December 16, 2025, the board voted 6-1 on a resolution addressing immigration enforcement and community safety. Board member Sherlett Hendy Newbill (District 1) cast the only dissenting vote.
